# Shape Hunt Process

## Why Shape Hunt exists

Before a designer edits curves in detail, they must see a large field of possibilities and develop taste pressure. The goal of Shape Hunt is rapid visual selection.

The app should feel closer to looking through contact sheets than using CAD.

## Funnel

### Stage 1 — Wide scan

Generate 500–1000 silhouettes for the 330 ml size. Show them as black profiles. The user quickly saves, rejects or asks for more like this.

### Stage 2 — Shortlist

Review saved candidates larger. Add tags: “has soul”, “dead”, “too camping”, “good foot”, “bad rim”.

### Stage 3 — A/B tournament

Pair candidates and ask which one is closer to the brand.

### Stage 4 — Family check

Apply the candidate DNA to 120 / 240 / 330 / 440. Reject candidates that fail as a family.

### Stage 5 — Handle exploration

Only after body silhouettes survive do handles enter the process.

### Stage 6 — Sculpt/CAD

Finalists move to a more technical geometry tool.

## Interaction rules

- The first screen must show many shapes.
- No sliders on the first screen.
- Decisions must be one click.
- Keyboard shortcuts are required.
- Every decision is recorded.
- “More like this” is more important than manual editing at this stage.

## Core actions

- GOOD — candidate has direction.
- BAD — candidate is rejected.
- SAVE — candidate belongs to shortlist.
- MORE LIKE THIS — generate around this candidate.
- A/B — choose which candidate is closer to the brand.
