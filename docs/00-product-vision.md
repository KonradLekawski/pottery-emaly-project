# Product Vision

## One sentence

A premium family enamelware platform that turns the everyday cup into a durable table object with heirloom character.

## What we are building

We are designing a family of enamel cups/filiżanki that belong at the table, not in the camping aisle. The product must work for children and adults without splitting the brand into “kids” and “grown-up” aesthetics.

The child receives a full member of the table family, adapted to hand size, weight, stability, safety and independence.

## Core line: Table Line

| Size | Role | Handles | Design role |
|---:|---|---:|---|
| 120 ml | first cup / toddler | 2 | stable, light, dignified, not toy-like |
| 240 ml | child | 2 | bridge between toddler and adult line |
| 330 ml | family daily | 1 | main brand DNA |
| 440 ml | large / parent | 1 | generous, but not bucket-like |

## Later platform line: Car Line

Car Line will be separate: cup-holder compatible, with lid, road-oriented, more vertical and technical. It must inherit the same brand DNA, but it should not force Table Line to become a travel mug.

## Design problem

The question is not “what cup shape is nice?”

The question is:

> What shape looks as if it has always belonged at a family table, but somehow has not been made yet?

## Non-goals

- Not a camping mug.
- Not a training cup.
- Not rustic nostalgia.
- Not cold minimalist tableware.
- Not a novelty object.
- Not a render-first exercise.

## Design filter

A candidate shape survives only if it answers yes to this:

> Could a child receive this as their first table object and still want the adult version years later?
